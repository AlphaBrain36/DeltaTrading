📊 Core Strategy: Order Flow Imbalance Detection

Monitoring 240 real-time streams across global capital flows to detect market imbalance before price discovery

The Trading Thesis

On expiry days, when options lose value rapidly, every small index move creates explosive reactions in option prices. The algorithm monitors cross-chain order flow imbalance across 240 real-time streams. When buy or sell pressure becomes extreme—and this imbalance is confirmed across all three indices (NIFTY, SENSEX, BANKNIFTY) simultaneously—the algo takes the opposite trade, targeting a mean-reversion snapback.

What is Order Flow Imbalance?

Order Flow Imbalance (OFI) occurs when the volume of buy orders significantly exceeds sell orders—or vice versa. When this imbalance becomes extreme, it signals one side has overextended. The market must adjust to match global pressure, causing prices to snap back in the opposite direction.

Why 240 Streams?

Reading across NIFTY, SENSEX, and BANKNIFTY simultaneously removes false signals caused by instrument-specific noise. When the imbalance pattern aligns across all three chains, conviction is highest and the trade is entered on the nearest expiry. This cross-chain confirmation separates real moves from noise.

Time-Based Option Selection (Expiry Day)

Before 2:00 PM
Out of the Money (OTM) Options

OTM options are cheap but highly reactive. When the market makes a sharp move, these can multiply several times in minutes.

Goal: Catch big spikes and exit fast with large profits
After 2:00 PM
In the Money / ATM Options

In final hours, cheap OTM options become a gamble. The algo switches to ITM or ATM options which retain value at settlement.

Goal: Stay protected, ensure recoverable value
Bank Nifty Monthly Exception

Bank Nifty monthly expiry falls on the last Thursday of each month. On that Thursday, the algo trades Bank Nifty instead of Sensex as it carries the nearest expiry and most aggressive price action of that cycle.
